Fig I (DPO accuracy: fraction of pairs DPO prefers chosen; length-biased). Cross-probe correlation between weight-structural metrics (srank, γ) and DPO reward margin. Left column: the in-distribution probe (hh-rlhf test) shows srank collapse is essentially decoupled from reward (Pearson r≈+0.28). Right column: on the out-of-distribution probe (UltraFeedback test), srank collapse becomes a strong negative predictor of reward margin (r≈−0.92), reversing the sign and amplifying the magnitude by ~3×. This asymmetry reframes the low-rank signature as representational overfitting rather than benign parameterization. n=5 Pythia LoRA-DPO checkpoints (70m/160m/410m/1b_s42/1b_s117, all trained on hh-rlhf); Qwen2-1.5B full-weight TRL-online-DPO on UltraFeedback shown as out-of-family reference point.
